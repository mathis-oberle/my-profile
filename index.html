SOSchool


Tutoring project

In April 2020 I launched a project called SOSchool with two classmates. The objective of this initiative was to provide a tutoring assistance to children from primary school with a migration background. At SOSchool, we thought these pupils to be the main indirect victims of the consecutive lockdowns and we believed that they were not paid enough attention to. Hence, we tried to rally numerous volunteering high-school students willing to tutor a child and through our close collaboration with the Luxembourgish social organisation Caritas and the Red Cross, we identified the pupils and families that required our help in priority. The main objective of the summer vacation in 2020 was to make sure that by the start of the next school year, they had catched up with the program. Indeed, Luxembourg being a country in which students are required to be fluent in four different languages, in the absence of the regular school program and parents who are proficient at these languages, many fell behind and have difficulties to keep up with their classmates. This experience was a great opportunity to contribute to the solving of a social issue and develop an entire set of new skills. Throughout this project, I had to work on my communication skills, master teamwork, find ways to energize those who worked with me and integrate strategies to overcome cultural dicrepancies. Furthermore, SOSchool confronted me to administrative challenges and initiated me to how to communicate with important partners.
